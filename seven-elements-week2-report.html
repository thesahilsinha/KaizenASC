05

What Went Wrong

Week 2's underperformance has a single root cause: inconsistent execution by the Kaizen ASC team. This is worth naming directly — not to assign blame, but because diagnosing the real problem is the only way to fix it. The strategy did not fail. The follow-through did.

LinkedIn outreach volume collapsed. Going from 24 messages in Week 1 to 8 in Week 2 is a 67% drop. At this stage of pipeline building, volume is non-negotiable. Every message not sent is a conversation that never starts. This is the most direct lever to pull.

Video content remained at zero. Video was flagged as the highest-leverage long-term channel after Week 1. Two weeks in, nothing has been published. Content compounds over time — every week without it is compounding delay. This needs a hard deadline and a simple brief, not a perfect plan.

No accountability mechanism in place. Without a daily or weekly activity tracker visible to both the client and the team, there is no early warning when effort drops. A simple shared dashboard or weekly check-in would have caught the outreach drop by mid-week.

06

Week 3 Action Plan

Week 3 is a correction week. The goal is not to invent new strategy — it is to execute the existing strategy at the volume it was always meant to run at. Five clear priorities.

→

LinkedIn outreach — 35+ messages

This is the non-negotiable floor. Prioritise targeted outreach to qualified contacts — decision-makers who show active signals of brand or design investment. Quality of targeting matters as much as volume now.

→

Cold email — 100+ sent with follow-up sequence

The Week 2 email metrics earned this. Scale to 100+ this week. Add a follow-up email for the ~21 people who opened but didn't click — they showed intent. A single follow-up can convert 15–25% of warm openers.

→

2 videos published — LinkedIn and/or Instagram

No more planning. One video this week does not need to be produced content — a direct-to-camera 60-second clip on a brand insight, a process reveal, or a client outcome is enough to start. Done beats perfect. Publish by Wednesday.

→

Cold calls — activate for warm email prospects

The 7 people who clicked through from the email campaign are the warmest leads in the entire pipeline right now. A short, confident cold call to each of them — referencing the email — converts at a meaningfully higher rate than cold calls without context.

↗

Targeted outreach for qualified contacts only

As volume increases, tighten the qualification criteria. Before sending any outreach — LinkedIn or email — the contact should meet at least two signals: decision-maker title, visible brand presence, or active hiring/growth activity. Less scatter, more precision.
