Core diagnosis: The ₹2L target is not just achievable — it is undersized relative to what Shreyash's work already justifies. The real ceiling is being held down by pricing, not by capability or by market access. This is a pricing and offer-architecture problem wearing the costume of a "lead generation" problem.
02 Market Landscape

The D2C and startup product-design market in India is expanding fast, fuelled by an explosion of online-first brands that need credible digital storefronts and product experiences but can't yet justify a large in-house design team.

India D2C startup ecosystem — active brands needing design/web partners (indexed, 2021–2026)
Index growing from 100 in 2021 to roughly 240 by 2026.
Market drivers
D2C funding rounds, quick-commerce growth, and Shopify/headless storefronts have created thousands of small-mid online brands that need polished websites and product UX but operate on lean budgets and fast timelines.
What this means for Resin
Demand for design partners in this segment is high, but it is also commoditised at the low end. Resin's actual leverage is not more leads — it's escaping the price band where every D2C founder assumes "₹40–50K" is the going rate.
03 The Pricing Gap

This is Finding One, and it is the most consequential number in this entire diagnostic. Resin is pricing its work roughly 60% below what the quality, scope, and craft genuinely support in this market.

Current price vs. true market value of comparable work
Current average deal at ₹42,000 versus true comparable market value of ₹1,00,000+.
Why this matters more than lead volume: At ₹42K, Shreyash needs roughly 5 projects a month to hit ₹2L — a volume-dependent, delivery-heavy model for a one-person-led shop less than 6 months old. At ₹1L, the same ₹2L target needs just 2 projects a month. Underpricing isn't just lost margin — it's the reason the business has to run faster than it should to stand still.
04 Why Calls Are Dropping at Enquiry

Finding Three. Shreyash reports getting meetings where prospects "understand the idea" but are already working with another agency — and the call still doesn't convert. The pattern points to one root cause: in the sales conversation, the lead holds all the power, and Resin holds none.

What's happening on the call today
No qualification before the call. No scripted framing of value. No anchor price stated with conviction. The prospect treats the call as a free consultation, compares Resin informally against their existing agency, and leaves without urgency to decide.
What needs to happen instead
A structured SOP: pre-call qualification, a fixed-offer framing presented with confidence, and a clear close mechanism. Power on the call comes from clarity and structure — not aggression.
The drop-off point being "enquiry" despite an informal funnel confirms this isn't a top-of-funnel problem. Leads are arriving. The conversation itself is where value is being lost.
05 The Treadmill Risk

Finding Three (continued). Resin sits in a grey zone common to early-stage agencies: enthusiastic, capable, and busy — but without the systems that convert effort into compounding growth. Left uncorrected, this grey zone has a known failure mode.

!
The window to fix this is narrow. If pricing, offer, and sales structure aren't corrected in the next 60–90 days, Resin risks becoming a "treadmill agency" — one where Shreyash trades hours for low-margin projects indefinitely, with revenue capped by his own time rather than by market demand. The longer underpricing persists, the harder the eventual price increase becomes, because the agency's reputation calcifies around the lower number.
06 Current Bottlenecks

A candid diagnosis of the structural gaps preventing Resin from converting its existing outreach effort into revenue.

Pricing doesn't match work quality
₹42K average deal size against work that performs at ₹1L+ standard. This single mismatch explains most of the revenue gap on its own.
No irresistible offer
Outreach effort is well-executed, but without a sharply packaged, hard-to-refuse offer, even good targeting produces lukewarm interest instead of urgency.
No power on the sales call
Leads and prospects currently control the conversation. Without a structured call framework, every call defaults to a price comparison the prospect controls.
No SOP for outreach-to-close
Cold outreach is running, but there is no documented, repeatable process connecting first touch to qualified call to signed deal. Every interaction is reinvented.
100% active, 0% passive acquisition
Cold outreach is the sole lead source. There is no passive or automated layer capturing inbound interest, meaning every lead costs founder time directly.
Lead quality, not just lead quantity
A share of current meetings are with prospects already committed to another agency — time spent on conversations with low structural likelihood of converting.
07 The Irresistible Offer — Three-Tier Architecture

Finding Two and the core strategic recommendation. Rather than one flat price point, Resin should operate three concurrent offer tiers — each serving a different purpose in the business.

Anchor offer
Tier 1 · Irresistible
Website Sprint Package
₹1,00,000
A tightly scoped, fast-turnaround website/design package built around Resin's most profitable service. Fixed price, fixed deliverables, fixed timeline — designed to be the easy "yes" that replaces the ₹42K negotiation entirely.
Tier 2 · Regular flow
Branding + Web Build
₹1L – ₹1.5L
The standard engagement for D2C startups needing both brand identity and a production-ready site. Keeps a steady base of recurring, predictable work flowing alongside the anchor offer.
Tier 3 · Whale accounts
Full-Stack Product Build
~₹2.5L+
For funded or scaling D2C brands needing full design + development. Lower volume, highest margin — the tier that turns a handful of accounts into the majority of monthly revenue.
Conservative pipeline math: 15 leads/month at the ₹1L anchor offer
15 leads at 1 lakh rupees creates a 15 lakh rupee pipeline. At 10 percent conversion that is 1.5 lakh rupees in monthly revenue.
The math: 15 leads/month × ₹1L offer = ₹15L of monthly pipeline value, even on the conservative end. A 10% close rate alone delivers ₹1.5L/month — already exceeding the current revenue and closing in on the stated ₹2L target from fewer, higher-value closes rather than higher volume.
